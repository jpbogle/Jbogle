I'm Jeremy Bogle, a Computer Science and Electrical Engineering major at MIT with a minor in Economics. I was born in Weston, MA and went to boarding school in Connecticut before coming to MIT. I have always been a dedicated and quick learner and excelled in math and science. When I came to MIT, I decided to major in computer science but also wanted to spend time learning economics and electrical engineering so I could apply the computer science theory I was learning. Throughout my undergraduate experience I expanded beyond the classroom and explored computer systems, web development, artificial intelligence and machine learning in various projects and internships. Applying the theory I learned in the classroom is how I became an engineer and what got me excited about learning everything MIT has to offer.

Outside of classes, I have always loved to stay active, playing sports and excercising. Being on both the water polo and swim teams at MIT taught me important leadership and team building skills. I also helped start a proffesional fraternity on campus that focuses on taking new members with an interest in proffesional development and providing them with the opportunity to live in a student managed and student led house of their closest friends.
